1. What Are Cookies

Cookies are small text files stored on your device when you visit a website. They help the site function correctly, remember your preferences, and allow us to analyse traffic and show relevant advertisements.

3. Why We Use Analytics & Advertising Cookies

We use Google Analytics to understand how visitors interact with our site — such as which pages are most visited and how long users stay. This helps us improve our content and user experience.

We use Meta Pixel and Google Ads to show relevant advertisements to people who have previously visited our website. This is known as retargeting. These cookies do not store directly identifiable personal information but use a unique identifier to recognise your browser or device.

4. Third-Party Data Processing

Analytics and advertising cookies are set by third parties (Google and Meta). Their data practices are governed by their own privacy policies:

  • Google Privacy Policy
  • Meta Privacy Policy

5. Managing Cookies

You can control or delete cookies through your browser settings. You can also opt out of interest-based advertising:

  • Google: adssettings.google.com
  • Meta: facebook.com/ads/preferences

Note: disabling certain cookies may affect how the website displays or limit ad personalisation, but will not prevent ads from being shown entirely.
